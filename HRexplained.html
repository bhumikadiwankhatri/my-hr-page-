  • Job Posting: XYZ Corp posts a job opening on various job portals and company website.
  • Application Screening: The HR team screens resumes to shortlist candidates who meet the job requirements.
  • Initial Interview: Shortlisted candidates are invited for initial interview to assess their fit for the role.
  • AptitudeTest: Candidates undergo an aptitude test to evaluate their problem-solving and analytical skills.
  • Technical Interview: Candidates who pass the aptitude test are invited for a technical interview with the department head.
  • HR Interview: A final HR interview is conducted to discuss salary, benefits, and cultural fit.
  • Job Offer: The selected candidate receives a job offer with details of the role, and other benefits.
  • Onboarding: Once the candidate accepts the offer, the HR team initiates the onboarding process to integrate the new employee into the company.

    • Tata Group's HR Management

    Tata Group is known for its employee-first policies. Their HR strategies emphasize long-term development, employee retention, and comprehensive training programs. Tata focuses on building leadership within the company through initiatives like Tata Leadership Development (TLD).

    Example: Tata encourages internal mobility by providing employees opportunities to switch roles within the company, thereby enhancing skillsets and experience. This creates a positive work environment and enhances job satisfaction.

    Amazon's HR Management

    Amazon's HR strategies are data-driven and employee-focused. Amazon uses innovative recruitment and performance management systems to keep their teams highly efficient and motivated. Amazon places great importance on leadership principles, promoting an intense and fast-paced work environment.

    Example: Amazon implements “Career Choice,” a program that pre-pays 95% of tuition for employees to pursue courses in in-demand fields, ensuring continuous learning and growth for their workforce.
